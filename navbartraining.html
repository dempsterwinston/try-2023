The Moon and its Phases

The Moon

The Moon is Earth's natural satellite, a rocky celestial body that orbits our planet. It is about 1/6th the size of Earth and has a barren, cratered surface with no atmosphere, water, or life. The Moon's gravitational pull causes tides on Earth, and it has played a significant role in human culture, astronomy, and space exploration, including serving as the site of several manned lunar missions.

Why does it change color?

The Moon's color can change due to atmospheric conditions on Earth. When there are particles and pollutants in the Earth's atmosphere, such as during a sunset or sunrise, the Moon can appear to take on a reddish or orange hue. This is because the Earth's atmosphere scatters shorter wavelengths of light, allowing longer wavelengths (like red and orange) to reach and illuminate the Moon, giving it a different color.
